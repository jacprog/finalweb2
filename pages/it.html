Becoming a programmer - Jacques

While studying horsemanship in the US, I become involved with producing web-based educational materials for a company leading the digital horsemanship community. The training company was very progressive using IT processes throughout most of their business. I was hooked and hungry to learn more.
I am now back in school at Santa Fe College to study.

Important design feature while building this web site

Responsive design

The site will adjust to the size of the screen. There is a desktop version and a mobile version.

Menu

The horizontal menu switches to a vertical menu on a small screen utilizing HTML 5 and CSS 3.

Parallax

On the Index page, three images slide across the screen and comments slide with a delay. Animation is achieved using jQuery by Dynamicdrive.com

Image rollover

On the Jaf page, images switch when you hover over a picture on the desktop view, and switch on touch when using a touch screen.

Carousel

On the bikes page, a carousel is powered by JQuerry. The contact page carouseli s powered by a more basic HTML coding.

Light box

On the Dior page, a five image JQuerry Lightbox swap is used. The thumbnails are resized to ensure they are inline regardless of screen size.

Forms

There is a google form HERE that is active.

Accordion

The Jacques page uses an accordion to display a list of facts about this site.

Links

The size of some of the links was increased and displayed in blue on the mobile screen size to make it easier to use on small touch screens.

More

On the Project page the Table is styled to fit on small screens. There are color effects as well as resizing of images and a change from one to two columns to adjust to different screen sizes. There is also a video on the Dior page. Last but not least, there is a live Google form HERE, in the event you want to give me feedback.
